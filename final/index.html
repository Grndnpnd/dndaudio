Getting Started

This audio control panel is designed to enhance your D&D Spooky Carnival campaign with immersive background music and sound effects. Here's how to use it:

Main Carnival Waltz

  • Play/Pause/Stop: Basic controls for the main carnival waltz background music.
  • Double Tempo: Increases the speed of the music to create tension or represent a more frantic carnival atmosphere.
  • Volume Control: Adjust the volume of just the main track.
DM Tip: Start the carnival waltz when players first enter the carnival grounds. The eerie waltz sets the mood for the entire area.

Special Effects

  • Shattering Floor: A glass breaking sound effect for when the floor beneath players begins to crack or shatter.
  • Violent Crescendo: Gradually increases the volume to build tension during important moments or revelations.
  • Twisted Rhythm: A warping, alien-like sound effect for when reality begins to distort in the carnival.
DM Tip: Use the "Shattering Floor" effect when players step onto unstable ground or when a glass illusion breaks. Use "Twisted Rhythm" when players encounter the carnival's true nature or when they're affected by mind-altering magic.

Master Controls

  • Master Volume: Controls the overall volume of all audio elements.
  • Mute All: Quickly silence all audio (useful for important announcements or when you need players' full attention).
  • Reset All: Returns all controls to their default state and stops all audio.

Suggested Usage During Campaign

  1. Begin with the main carnival waltz playing at a moderate volume as players explore.
  2. Use the "Double Tempo" feature when chase scenes occur or when tension rises.
  3. Trigger the "Shattering Floor" effect when players encounter the breaking mirror maze or crumbling pathways.
  4. Use "Violent Crescendo" during the build-up to the final confrontation with the carnival master.
  5. Play the "Twisted Rhythm" effect when reality warps or when players enter the distorted dimensions of the carnival.
Technical Note: If audio doesn't play immediately, try clicking anywhere on the page first. Some browsers require user interaction before allowing audio playback.
